# Manuscript letter — "Beloved friend May" (page 2)

long labored — and labored not in vain. That we three should have been preserved to witness what our eyes now behold is matter of personal congratulation and heartfelt thankfulness. May it be ours to see the jubilee before "this mortal shall have put on immortality"!

We have been considering your kind and urgent invitation to have Fanny, wife and myself make you and your dear wife a visit at Syracuse; and we have agreed upon the following programme, trusting the time named for us to be in S. will not be inconvenient to you:—

(1) Leave Boston Thursday morning next, June 11th, and spend the night in Utica.

(2) Friday morning, 12th, go to Trenton Falls, and spend the night there.

(3) Saturday, 13th, go to Peterboro', via Canastota. [I have written to Mr. Smith to this effect.]

(4) Remain in Peterboro' until Wednesday morning, 17th, when we shall go to Syracuse,
